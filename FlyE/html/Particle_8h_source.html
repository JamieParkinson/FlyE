1 /*
2  * Particle class for FlyE
3  *
4  * Each instance of this class represents one particle. The type of the position/velocity variables is templated as T. Each particle has the following properties:
5  * Position - r_ (3d vector)
6  * Velocity - v_ (3d vector)
7  * Trajectory storage - rTrajectory_ and vTrajectory_ (FLATTENED vectors; have to be accessed as pseudomultidimensional)
8  * Principal quantum no. - n_ (int)
9  * Stark quantum no. - k_ (int)
10  * Status - collided_, ionised_ and succeeded_ are bools which are true if the particle has collided with an electrode,
11  * been ionised or reached the end of the accelerator, respectively.
12  *
13  * We can construct the particle with no arguments at rest at the origin, or with (x, y, z, vx, vy, vz). Calling memorise() at any time commits the current position and velocity
14  * trajectory vectors. Various overloads of setLoc and setVel, and getLoc and getVel, can change/get the location and velocity. Similarly recallLoc and recallVel can access the trajectory
15  * vectors. collide(), ionise(), and succeed() change the status variables, and we can check the status with isDead().
16  *
17  * IMPORTANT: Note that, in order to conserve memory, the trajectory vectors are deleted if the particle collides or ionises.
18  *
19  * Jamie Parkinson, July 2014
20  */
